Power Tracker for Schools — Summary

  • Make energy usage visible during everyday browsing.
  • Turn real numbers into math/science/Green Team projects.
  • Teach digital citizenship through responsible tech use.
Implementation Steps

Why This Matters for Students

Links Technology Habits to Real-World Impact

Most students don't realize that every search, video, or game uses electricity. Power Tracker makes this visible in real time, connecting everyday browsing to the school's sustainability goals.

Creates Data Students Can Use in Class & Clubs

The extension generates actual numbers on energy use. These can become:
• Math projects: graphing daily/weekly browser energy trends
• Science experiments: comparing which activities use more energy
• Green Team campaigns: posters, contests, or challenges based on real data

Builds Digital Citizenship & Responsibility

Schools already teach recycling and conservation in the building. Power Tracker extends that lesson into the digital world, helping students understand their role as responsible technology users.

Supports Project-Based Learning (PBL)

Teachers can integrate Power Tracker into lessons:
• Social studies → global energy & climate topics
• Computer science → efficiency of sites/apps
• Environmental science → connecting online energy habits with carbon footprint

Gives the School a Leadership Edge

Adopting Power Tracker lets your school say:
• "We use digital tools to teach energy awareness."
• Highlight in newsletters, assemblies, or sustainability reports.

What Power Tracker Can Do

  • Show estimated browser energy use in real time
  • Visualize simple trends for the day/week
  • Encourage reflection on high-energy activities
  • Provide data students can graph or summarize
  • Run without student accounts or personal data
  • Work within Chrome's standard extension controls

How Schools Implement It

  1. Step 1 — Review

    Principal/Advisor reviews this page for goals, safety, and classroom fit.

  2. Step 2 — Request

    Advisor emails district IT to whitelist & push the extension to managed Chromebooks.

  3. Step 3 — Pilot

    Green Team or a class pilots for 2–4 weeks; students use data in small projects.

  4. Step 4 — Share

    Publish a short summary (newsletter/assembly) highlighting learning outcomes.

    If district policy cannot push to devices, students can install at home from the Chrome Web Store.

Privacy & Safety

Power Tracker for Schools is designed with student privacy and safety as the top priority:

  • No ads, no trackers on this page
  • No student accounts required
  • Data stays in the browser unless a student exports it
  • Built to comply with Chrome Web Store policies

Student FAQ

Will this slow my Chromebook?

No! Power Tracker is designed to be lightweight and won't affect your browsing experience or device performance.

Does it collect my name or personal info?

Absolutely not. The extension doesn't collect any personal information - it only tracks anonymous energy usage data that stays on your device.

How do I remove it?

You can easily remove the extension through Chrome's standard extension controls - just go to Settings → Extensions and click Remove.

Teacher & IT FAQ

Installation Requirements

Installation is only available through the Chrome Web Store - no sideloading required. The extension follows all Chrome Web Store policies and security guidelines.

Privacy Compliance

No personally identifiable information (PII) is needed for the extension to function. All data processing happens locally on the student's device.

Educational Value

Helps students understand digital energy consumption and develops awareness of sustainable technology practices.
